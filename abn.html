Part 1 — Understand

TFN vs ABN — what’s the difference?

Most backpackers have a TFN. Fewer understand when they also need an ABN — and why it matters.

TFN work (employee)

You work for a company. They pay you a wage. They handle your tax automatically through payroll. You get a payslip. TFN is all you need.

Tax is withheld from every pay. You lodge a tax return at year end to reconcile — and usually get a refund.

Examples: hospitality, retail, warehouse, office, farm work as an employee.

ABN work (sole trader)

You work for yourself or contract directly to a business. You invoice them. They pay the full amount — no tax withheld.

You’re responsible for setting aside your own tax. At year end you declare this income in your tax return and pay what’s owed.

Examples: farm piece-rate, festival crew, freelance, cleaning, deliveries.

Common mistake: Many farmers and festivals insist on paying ABN. If you give them your TFN instead, they’re required to withhold 47%. Get the ABN to get paid in full.
Part 2 — How to register

Registering your ABN — step by step.

The Australian Business Register (ABR) is the official free registration point. Takes 5 minutes if you have your details ready.

What you’ll need
Your TFN — must have this first
Passport details and date of birth
Australian address (hostel is fine)
Description of your business activity (e.g. “farm labouring”, “hospitality services”)
Email address — ABN confirmation sent here
The process
Step 1
Go to abr.gov.au and click “Apply for an ABN”
Step 2
Select “Sole trader” as your business structure
Step 3
Enter your details — use your legal name exactly as it appears on your passport
Done
ABN issued instantly or within 20 days

Part 4 — Tax obligations

ABN income and tax — what you actually owe.

The biggest mistake with ABN work is not setting aside money for tax. Nobody withholds it for you — you’re responsible.

15%

On your first $45,000 of ABN income. Set this aside from every invoice payment so you’re not caught out at tax time.

The golden rule
Set aside 15–20% of every payment

Put it in a separate account. Touch it only at tax time. This one habit prevents every ABN tax surprise.

No withholding = your responsibility. The ATO will catch up at tax time. Underpaying can trigger interest and penalties. Keep records of every invoice you send and every payment you receive.
Questions

Things everyone actually asks

Do I need an ABN for cash jobs?
If the payer is a business and the amount is over $75, technically yes. Most small cash payments between individuals don’t require an ABN, but farm and festival work typically does. When in doubt, register — it’s free and protects you.
Can I have both a TFN and an ABN?
Yes — and many backpackers do. You work a standard job (TFN) and do some farm or freelance work (ABN) at the same time. All income goes into one tax return at the end of the year. We handle mixed returns all the time.
What if I earn under $75,000 — do I need to charge GST?
No. GST registration is only required when your annual ABN income exceeds $75,000. Most backpackers never hit this threshold, so you invoice without GST.
Can I cancel my ABN when I leave Australia?
Yes. You can cancel it online through the ABR at any time. There’s no penalty for cancelling, and no obligation to keep it active after you stop working.
The farm wants to pay me ABN but I don’t have one yet. What do I do?
Register now at abr.gov.au — it takes 5 minutes and is usually approved instantly. If they insist before you have it, they can withhold 47% until you provide your ABN. Don’t let that happen.
