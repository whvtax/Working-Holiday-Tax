Part 1 — Understand

What is Medicare —
and what does it cost you?

Before you worry about it, understand what you’re dealing with. Medicare is simpler than it sounds.

In plain English

Medicare is Australia’s public healthcare system — it covers GP visits, hospital treatment, and some medicines. It’s funded by a 2% levy on taxable income, collected through the tax system.

Here’s the key thing most guides miss: Medicare access and Medicare levy are two different things. You can be exempt from paying the levy without having access to Medicare services.

The ATO calculates your Medicare levy automatically. If you don’t apply for the exemption, they assume you’re liable. Applying is how you get that money back.

The exemption isn’t automatic. The ATO defaults to charging the levy. We apply this exemption as part of every tax return we lodge — you don’t have to think about it.

Questions

Things everyone actually asks

I already lodged my return and didn’t claim the exemption. Can I fix it?
Yes. You can amend a tax return up to 2 years after lodgment. If you paid Medicare levy you weren’t liable for, we can lodge an amendment and recover the difference. This is worth doing — it adds up.
I’m from the UK. Do I pay the Medicare levy?
It depends on your specific situation. UK nationals have an RHCA agreement — so if you enrolled in Medicare, you may owe the levy. If you didn’t enrol, you may be exempt. We assess this individually for every UK client.
My employer has been deducting Medicare levy from my payslips. Do I get it back?
Quite possibly, yes. If you’re eligible for the exemption, the levy withheld during the year comes back to you as part of your tax refund. This is one of the most common refund components we recover.
What if I had Medicare for part of the year and not the rest?
The levy is pro-rated based on the days you were eligible. We calculate the exact number of days and apply the exemption for the portion of the year you weren’t covered.
Is the exemption included in your standard tax return fee?
Yes. The Medicare levy exemption is included in every return we lodge — not an add-on, not an extra charge. It’s standard practice.
