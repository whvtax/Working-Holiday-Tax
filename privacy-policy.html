Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2024 · The Accounting Academy Pty Ltd ABN 26 669 927 959
Plain English summary

We collect your personal and tax information to do one thing: prepare and lodge your Australian tax return. We never sell your data. We never share it except where the law requires. You can ask us to delete it at any time.

01

What information we collect

To prepare your tax return or super claim, we collect: your full name, date of birth, and contact details; your Tax File Number (TFN); employment and income details; bank account details; passport and visa information; and any receipts or documents you send us.

When you visit our website, we also collect standard non-identifying data like IP address and pages visited. This helps us improve the site.

02

How we use your information

We use your information solely to provide the services you’ve requested: preparing and lodging your tax return, processing your super claim, and communicating with you about your return.

We will never sell your information or share it with advertisers. Your data exists to complete your return — nothing else.
03

Who we share it with

We share your information only with: the ATO (to lodge your return), the Tax Practitioners Board (if required for compliance), your super fund (for DASP claims), and trusted subcontractors bound by the same confidentiality obligations.

That’s it. No data brokers, no marketing partners, no third parties beyond what’s needed.

04

How we keep it safe

Our security measures include SSL encryption, secure digital storage with restricted access, and confidentiality obligations for all staff and contractors.

We will never ask for your myGov username or password. We access ATO records through the official registered tax agent portal. If anyone claiming to be us asks for these details, do not provide them.
05

How long we keep it

Australian tax law requires registered agents to retain client records for a minimum of 5 to 7 years. After this period, your data is securely deleted or anonymised.

06

Your rights

Under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), you have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal information, and to opt out of marketing communications at any time. Contact us on WhatsApp or by email to exercise any of these rights.

07

Complaints

If you have concerns about how we handle your personal information, contact us directly first. If unsatisfied, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.
