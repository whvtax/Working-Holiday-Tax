Part 1 — Understand

What is superannuation —
and why do you have some?

Super is Australia’s mandatory retirement savings system. For backpackers, it works differently — you can claim it all back when you leave.

How it works

Australian law requires every employer to pay 12% of your gross salary into a super fund on your behalf — on top of your wages, not taken from them.

If you earned $1,000 in a week, your employer also paid $120 directly into a super account. You might never have seen this money — but it’s been accumulating the entire time you worked.

For Working Holiday Makers, a process called DASP (Departing Australia Superannuation Payment) lets you withdraw it all when you leave permanently.

Part 2 — The tax

Yes — there’s a tax on the withdrawal.
Here’s how much.

The ATO withholds tax when you claim DASP. The rate is high — but you’re still getting back significant money that’s otherwise left in Australia forever.

Questions

Things everyone actually asks

I left Australia 3 years ago and never claimed. Is it too late?
No — it’s never too late. After 6 months of inactivity, funds transfer to the ATO under your TFN. It’s still 100% yours. Don’t leave it any longer.
I don’t know which super fund I’m with. Can you still help?
Yes. This is very common. We locate all super funds linked to your TFN using ATO tools — including funds you may have forgotten about from previous employers. Often we find more than the client knew they had.
Can I claim super and a tax refund at the same time?
Yes — and many backpackers do. We handle both together. Your tax return and your DASP are separate processes but we run them in parallel to save time.
I worked farm jobs for cash. Do I have super?
If the farm paid you as an employee, they were legally required to pay super. Whether they did is another matter — but it’s always worth checking. Cash payment doesn’t automatically mean no super was paid.
How much do you charge?
Our fee is a percentage of the super amount recovered — confirmed before we start. You pay only when the money arrives in your account. No upfront payment, no surprises.
