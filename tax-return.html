Part 1 — Understand

What actually is a tax return?

Most people have heard of it. Almost nobody understands it before they do their first one.

In plain English

Australia runs a financial year from 1 July to 30 June. During that year, your employer withholds tax from every payslip based on an estimate.

At year end, you lodge a tax return — it tells the ATO exactly what you earned. They calculate your actual tax, compare it to what was withheld, and send you the difference.

For most Working Holiday Makers, the estimate was too high — meaning the ATO owes you money. But it doesn’t arrive automatically. You have to claim it.

Key dates
Financial year begins — 1 July
Year ends, data finalised — 14 July
Lodge your return — by 31 October
Refund in your account — 7–14 days

Using a registered tax agent extends the deadline — giving you more time even after leaving Australia.

The refund doesn’t come automatically. The ATO holds it until you lodge. Many backpackers leave without knowing this — and lose the money entirely.

Part 3 — Deductions

Deductions — money most people leave behind.

Every deduction reduces your taxable income. Each $100 of deductions adds $15 to your refund. Here are the most common ones backpackers miss.

Work uniforms & protective clothing
Clothing required for work that you couldn’t wear normally outside of it.
Safety boots, hi-vis vests, non-slip shoes, branded uniforms
Receipt if over $300
Licences & certifications
Licences you paid for that were required for your specific job.
RSA, White Card, forklift licence, Working With Children
Must be job-required
Tools & equipment
Tools you bought yourself and used for work — not supplied by your employer.
Hand tools, kitchen knives (chefs), gardening equipment
Under $300 — full deduction
Laundry costs
Washing and drying costs for work uniforms you’re required to maintain yourself.
$1 per load (washing + drying), $0.50 washing only
ATO fixed rate — no receipts needed
Keep every receipt. The ATO can ask you to prove any deduction up to 5 years after you lodge. A photo on your phone is enough. No receipt = no deduction.

Questions

Things everyone actually asks

I already left Australia. Can I still get my refund?
Yes. You can lodge from anywhere in the world. The refund goes to any bank account you nominate — including accounts in your home country. This is one of our most common scenarios.
I worked for multiple employers. What do I do?
Every employer goes in the same return. We pull all employer records from the ATO using your TFN — you don’t need to chase payslips from each one.
I worked both TFN and ABN jobs. Is that one return?
Yes — one return covers everything. TFN and ABN income are both included in a single lodgment. We handle mixed returns all the time.
I haven’t lodged for 3 years. Is it too late?
No. You can lodge up to 5 years of back returns. We regularly lodge 2–4 years at once for backpackers who didn’t realise they were owed money.
What happens if I don’t lodge?
The ATO applies a Failure to Lodge penalty of $330 for every 28 days overdue, up to $1,650. Beyond that, your refund sits with the ATO unclaimed. Using a registered agent extends your deadline.
How much do you charge?
Our fee is $200 + GST for a standard tax return. Complex returns are quoted before you commit. You pay only when the return is lodged — never upfront.
