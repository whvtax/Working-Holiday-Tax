What is a TFN?

Your Australian tax ID

A Tax File Number is a 9-digit number issued by the ATO. Every employer needs it to tax you correctly. Without it, they must withhold 47% of every dollar you earn.

In plain English

A Tax File Number is a 9-digit ID that the Australian Tax Office (ATO) gives you. Think of it like a social security number, but just for taxes.

Every time you earn money in Australia, your employer uses this number to work out how much tax to take from your pay.

Without it, the law requires them to take 47% of every dollar you earn. With it, Working Holiday Makers pay just 15%.

Common questions

The things everyone actually asks

Can I start work before I have my TFN?
Yes — for up to 28 days. After that, your employer must withhold 47% until you provide it. Apply as soon as you arrive.
Do I need a permanent address in Australia?
No. A hostel, friend’s address, or PO Box works — as long as you’ll be there when the letter arrives (within 28 days).
What if I lose my TFN?
Call the ATO on 13 28 61 (Mon–Fri 8am–6pm). They’ll verify your identity and give you your number over the phone.
Can I get a TFN on a tourist visa?
No. You need a valid work visa (417 or 462). Applying on a tourist visa will be rejected.
I got my TFN — what’s next?
Give it to your employer. When the financial year ends on 30 June, you’ll need to lodge a tax return to get back any overpaid tax. We handle that for you.
