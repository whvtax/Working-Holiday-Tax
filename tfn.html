Part 1 — Understand

What is a TFN — in plain English?

A Tax File Number is a 9-digit ID the ATO gives you. Every employer needs it to tax you at the correct rate. Without it, they must withhold 47% of every dollar you earn — by law.

How it works

Your TFN is yours for life. You get one number, and it follows you from job to job, city to city, and even if you return to Australia on a future visa.

When you give your TFN to an employer, they can apply the correct 15% Working Holiday Maker tax rate instead of the emergency 47% rate.

Applying is always free. Any website charging a fee for TFN applications is unnecessary — the ATO form is free and takes 10 minutes.

Questions

Things everyone actually asks

Can I start work before I have my TFN?
Yes — for up to 28 days. After that, your employer must withhold 47% until you provide it. Apply as soon as you arrive in Australia.
Do I need a permanent address in Australia?
No. A hostel, friend’s address, or any address where you can receive post works — as long as you’ll be there within the next 28 days.
What if I lose my TFN?
Call the ATO on 13 28 61 (Mon–Fri 8am–6pm AEST). They’ll verify your identity and give you your number over the phone.
Can I get a TFN on a tourist visa?
No. You need a valid work visa (417 or 462). Applying on a tourist visa will be rejected by the ATO.
I got my TFN — what’s next?
Give it to your employer. When the financial year ends on 30 June, you’ll need to lodge a tax return to get back any overpaid tax. We handle that for you.
